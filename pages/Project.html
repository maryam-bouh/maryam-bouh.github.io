Pomodoro Timer

1. Creating a pomodoro timer. The "Pomodoro Method" is a simple method to balance focus with deliberate breaks it consists of a 25 minute study session and a buzzer sound will go off and you will begin the 5 minute schedule break. To get ideal results you would repeat the process 3 more times then take a long break after the 4th. We built this device with the idea of helping students improve their study habits by committing to this technique of work. Since we are engineering students and experience procrastination, the opportunity to create a tool that would help ourselves as well as others was a chance that we couldnâ€™t miss.

Finished Product

Our pomodoro timer was created by conconnecting the wall output to the barrel jack. Taking this 12v in from our wall plug we step this voltage down to 5v to act as our Vdd for our components. Our circuit begins with our AVR, this is powered on and awaiting a signal to begin counting. Once the signal has been given it will begin to increment until a specified timer value is reached. Once this timer is reached a reset call is made for our second timer before executing a reset to the screen, the second timer will begin to increment until it's timer value at which counter 1 will reset. An alarm will sound, and the screen will reset with the loop.
