ENCM 369

Computer Orginaztion

ENCM 369 was an exciting class I took this semester! It was very well structured, and I felt as if I gained the most experience from this class. In this class I learned about micro controllers and designed projects based on concepts we learned in the class. Skills

  • Programing blinking LEDs
  • Reading from data sheets
  • Using Pickit Programmers
  • Programming Microchips

AVR Micro-Chip

The labs were also well rounded allowing us to do work beyond what was expected from us, with the help of the AVR data sheet.
When I was first introduced to micro controllers I was unable to wrap my head around how the AVR128DB28 worked, and was totally oblivious to the amount of projects it can handle.
However, despite the coding being very hard it made a lot of sense at the end.

A particular interest in a lab was where I designed an Analog to Digital convertor to sample an analog input voltage level and convert it to a number.
We then used The ADC to turn on different LEDs depending on the voltage level that was applied to pin PD2 on the AVR.
